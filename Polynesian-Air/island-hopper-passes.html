Island Hopper Passes

Hawaii Pass

Big Island, Maui, Oahu, Kauai

Hawaii always tops travelers’ lists, and the islands have become almost mythical in many would-be visitors’ eyes. There are a total of eight main islands in the Hawaiian archipelago in the North Pacific, though Niihau and Kahoolawe are forbidden to visitors. Of the remaining six—Hawaii Island, Kauai, Lanai, Maui, Molokai and Oahu—each island is unique in its ambiance and attractions, and all are perfect winter travel destinations.

From: $599

Society Islands Pass

Tahiti, Moorea, Huahine, Raiatea, Bora Bora, Maupiti

The Society Islands are a group of "high islands" which, besides Tahiti (the biggest in French Polynesia), include Moorea, an island with luxuriant gardens delimited by two magnificent bays, Huahine with its wild landscape and rich archeological heritage, and Raiatea, "the sacred isle", the cradle of Polynesian culture. From Raiatea, you can go by boat to Tahaa, a preserved island with authentic charm.

From: $699

Samoa Pass

Upolu and Savai'i

Samoa's two main islands, Upolu and Savai’i, are surrounded by several smaller islands, including Manono and Apolima - the next largest two. Most visitors to Samoa will arrive on Upolu first,which is the location of Samoa's capital Apia and its international airport and port. Many also travel to Savai’i, the biggest island in the country but sparsely populated, and a few will make it to small, deeply traditional Manono Island.

From: $179

Cook Islands Pass

Rarotonga, Aitutaki, Atiu

The Cook Islands is a nation in the South Pacific, with political links to New Zealand. Its 15 islands are scattered over a vast area. The largest island, Rarotonga, is home to rugged mountains and Avarua, the national capital. To the north, Aitutaki Island has a vast lagoon encircled by coral reefs and small, sandy islets. The country is renowned for its many snorkeling and scuba-diving sites.

From: $399

Tonga Pass

Tongatapu, Kao, ʿEua, Late Island, Vavaʿu

With over 170 islands full of wonder and un-spoilt beauty, there are plenty of unforgettable adventures on offer for any traveler in the Kingdom of Tonga. Journey back in time to experience the Kingdom of Tonga’s authentic and compelling mix of centuries-old culture,history and traditions. Understand why Tonga’s relaxing combination of uncrowded, unhurried and undiscovered is the “True South Pacific”.

From: $789

French Polynesia Atolls Pass

Tikehau, Rangiroa, Fakarava

The enchanting Tuamotu Archipelago consists mainly of atolls, ring-shaped coral reefs encircling a lagoon whose colours range through every possible shade of green and blue. Veritable natural aquariums, these islands are ideal for watersports and diving.

From: $959

Austral Islands Pass

Rimatara, Rurutu, Tubuai, Raivavae

To the southwest of Tahiti lie the Austral Islands. Lands of tradition, the Austral Islands' appeal lies in their preserved nature and authentic welcome. For lovers of marine animals, humpback whales offer the luckier visitors an unforgettable spectacle between July and October.

From: $1199

Marquesas Islands Pass

Nuku Hiva, Hiva Oa

Very close to the Equator, 1,500 km (932 mi) from Tahiti, the Marquesas Islands were made famous by Gauguin and Jacques Brel. For travellers in search of "a different Polynesia", the Marquesas Islands, also known as the "Land of Men", offer some powerful, majestic scenery, dotted with wild horses and goats. Choosing the Marquesas also means discovering some very rich cultural heritage.

From: $1149
